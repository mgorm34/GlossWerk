I've been building something for the past few months that I'm finally ready to share.

GlossWerk is a DE→EN patent translation tool with built-in quality triage. Every translated segment gets automatically rated — publishable, needs a quick look, or needs real editing — so reviewers only spend time where it matters.

Why patents specifically? Because patent translation has a unique combination of problems that generic MT+PE doesn't solve well: deeply nested German syntax, domain-specific terminology that shifts between patents, and the reality that most segments in a good translation don't need human review at all.

In our testing, about two-thirds of segments come through as publishable without edits. The rest get flagged with specific issues and suggested fixes.

The pipeline includes:
→ Automatic terminology extraction and glossary enforcement
→ Structural analysis for complex German constructions
→ Quality estimation that catches calques, redundancies, and readability issues
→ One-click fixes with highlighted changes

I'm offering free demo access to LSPs and translation teams that handle German patent work. 3 patents, no strings attached — I want feedback from people who actually do this work.
