## Translation Strategy Notes

### Cognates & Latin Terms
- **Annulus**: Use as-is (Latin cognate, standard in English medical literature)
- **Chordae tendineae**: Preserve as Latin anatomical term (standard English medical usage)
- **NiTiNol**: Preserve as proprietary brand/material designation

### Compound Term Analysis
- **Mitralklappenstent**: Break into components for clarity: "mitral valve stent" (not "mitral stent" alone, which loses specificity)
- **Stentbügel**: "Stent strut" is preferred in cardiac device terminology (more precise than "arm")
- **Herzklappenprothese**: "Heart valve prosthesis" maintains distinction from native "herzklappe"

### Directional/Anatomical Terms
- **proximal/distal, atrial/ventrikulär**: Use standard English anatomical directional terminology
- **Segel/Mitralklappensegel**: "Leaflet" is standard English cardiology terminology for valve flaps

### Material & Mechanism Terms
- **Formgedächtnislegierung**: "Shape-memory alloy" (standard technical English)
- **Selbstexpansion**: "Self-expansion" or "self-expanding" depending on context
- **Entfaltung**: "Deployment" preferred in device context over "unfolding"

---

## Consistency Recommendations

1. **Maintain the three pre-selected terms** as specified (stent, cartridge, heart valve)
2. **Use compound form** for "mitral valve stent" rather than abbreviations
3. **Distinguish** between structural elements using "strut" (stent) vs. "leaflet" (valve)
4. **Preserve Latin/anatomical terms** where standard in English medical literature
5. **Technical translation** of material and mechanism terms using established English terminology
