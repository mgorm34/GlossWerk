# German-English Terminology Review
## Mitral Valve Stent Patent (DE 10 2006 052 564 B3)

### Overview
This document catalogs key German medical and technical terminology extracted from a German patent on a collapsible mitral valve stent. The terminology spans cardiology, anatomy, surgical procedures, and device engineering. Recommended English translations prioritize consistency with established medical literature and FDA/international regulatory terminology.

---

## Primary Device and Component Terms

### **Stent Structure**
- **Mitralklappenstent** → *Mitral valve stent*
  - Preferred over "mitral valve stent prosthesis" in this context, as the valve is integrated separately
  - Appears 40+ times; critical for consistency

- **Stentbügel** → *Stent strut* OR *Stent arm*
  - "Strut" is more technical (preferred in engineering contexts)
  - "Arm" is more anatomical (preferred in clinical contexts)
  - Recommendation: Use **"stent strut"** for technical descriptions; reserve "arm" for anatomical positioning
  - Appears with variants: Stentbügel, Stützbügel (support strut)

- **Verankerungselemente** → *Anchoring elements* OR *Anchor hooks*
  - Used for proximal (atrial) fixation
  - "Anchoring elements" is more neutral; "anchor hooks" emphasizes the mechanical function
  - Recommendation: **"Anchoring elements"** for component descriptions

- **Klappenstent** → *Valve stent* OR *Stented valve*
  - Compound term referring to integrated stent-valve unit
  - Recommendation: **"Valve stent"** (preserves German word order convention)

## Anatomical Components

### **Mitral Valve Anatomy**
- **Mitralklappe** → *Mitral valve* (standard)

- **anteriores Mitralklappensegel** / **vorderes Mitralklappensegel** → *Anterior mitral valve leaflet* OR *Anterior mitral leaflet*
  - "Leaflet" is standard medical terminology
  - Appears frequently; critical for clarity
  - Abbreviation in text: AMKS

- **posteriores Mitralklappensegel** / **hinteres Mitralklappensegel** → *Posterior mitral valve leaflet* OR *Posterior mitral leaflet*
  - "Leaflet" is standard medical terminology
  - Abbreviation in text: PMKS

- **Mitralklappenannulus** / **Mitralannulus** / **Annulus** → *Mitral annulus* OR *Mitral valve annulus*
  - "Mitral annulus" is the established international term
  - Alternative: *Mitral valve ring* (less common in English)
  - Recommendation: **"Mitral annulus"** (consistent with EVAR/endovascular terminology)

- **Sehnenfäden** / **Chordae tendineae** → *Chordae tendineae* OR *Chordal structures* OR *Tendinous chords*
  - Latin term "Chordae tendineae" is used in English medical literature
  - "Chordal structures" is more accessible
  - Recommendation: **"Chordae tendineae"** (for technical/anatomical contexts); **"chordal structures"** (for clinical descriptions)

- **Papillarmuskel** → *Papillary muscle*
  - Standard anatomical term
  - Variants: anteriorer Papillarmuskel (anterior papillary muscle), posteriorer Papillarmuskel (posterior papillary muscle)

### **Cardiac Chambers and Adjacent Structures**
- **linker Vorhof** → *Left atrium*
  - Abbreviation: LA (used in text)

- **linke Herzkammer** / **linker Ventrikel** → *Left ventricle*
  - Abbreviation: LV (used in text)

- **Aortenklappe** → *Aortic valve*
  - Standard term

- **Ausflusstrakt** → *Outflow tract* OR *Left ventricular outflow tract*
  - Context-dependent: LVOT is common abbreviation in TAVI/interventional cardiology

## Pathophysiological and Clinical Concepts

### **Conditions and Complications**
- **Herzklappensklerose** / **Klappersklerose** → *Valve sclerosis* OR *Valve calcification*
  - Patent uses both; "calcification" is more clinically specific in this context

- **Klappverkalkung** → *Valve calcification*
  - More specific than sclerosis

- **Sam-Phänomen** / **Systolic Anterior Movement** → *Systolic Anterior Movement (SAM)* OR *SAM phenomenon*
  - English acronym is standard; keep both German and English designations

- **Linksherz-Dekompensation** → *Left heart decompensation* OR *Left ventricular decompensation*
  - More specific: **"Left ventricular decompensation"**

### **Hemodynamics**
- **Blutfluss** / **Durchfluss** → *Blood flow*
  - "Blutfluss" = blood flow; "Durchfluss" = flow/throughput

- **Vorwärts-Drängen** → *Forward displacement* OR *Forward prolapse*
  - Context-specific; in this case: "anterior displacement" or "systolic anterior movement"

## Surgical and Procedural Terms

### **Surgical Approach**
- **perkutane Implantation** / **perkutan** → *Percutaneous implantation* / *Percutaneously*
  - Standard medical term

- **minimal-invasiv** / **Minimal-invasive Operation** → *Minimally invasive* / *Minimally invasive procedure*
  - Standard terminology

- **endovaskulär** → *Endovascular* OR *Endovasculary*
  - Standard in interventional radiology

- **Katheter** → *Catheter*
  - Also: Kathetersystem (catheter system)

- **Kartusche** → *Cartridge* OR *Catheter cartridge*
  - Device-specific term; refers to the loading/delivery cartridge
  - Recommendation: **"Delivery cartridge"** (context in device deployment)

### **Surgical Actions**
- **Implantation** → *Implantation* (standard)
  - Common in patent context

- **Entfaltung** / **entfalten** → *Deployment* / *To deploy* OR *Expansion* / *To expand*
  - Mechanical context: **"deployment"**
  - Physiological context: **"expansion"**

- **Zusammenfalten** / **Faltung** → *Folding* / *To fold* OR *Collapsing* / *To collapse*
  - In delivery context: **"Collapse"** is preferred (e.g., "collapsed state," "to collapse")
  - Recommendation: Use **"collapsed state"** and **"to collapse"** for delivery systems

- **Einrollen** / **Einrollung** → *Curling* / *To curl* OR *Coiling* / *To coil* OR *Rolling up* / *To roll up*
  - Patient-specific (curling of the valve leaflet)
  - Device-specific (rolling mechanism)
  - Recommendation: **"To curl"** / **"curling"** for leaflet action; **"to roll"** / **"rolling"** for mechanical spiraling action

- **Einpacken** / **einklappen** → *Tucking* / *To tuck* OR *Folding* / *To fold under*
  - Describes displacement of valve leaflet
  - Recommendation: **"Tucking"** (more surgical terminology)

- **Verankerung** / **verankern** → *Anchoring* / *To anchor* OR *Fixation* / *To fix*
  - Recommendation: **"Anchoring"** / **"to anchor"** (emphasizes the mechanism)

- **Verdrängen** → *To displace* OR *To push aside*
  - Context: pushing the old valve leaflet out of the way
  - Recommendation: **"Displacement"** / **"to displace"**

- **Zurückdrängen** → *To push back* OR *To retract*
  - Slightly more specific than "displace"
  - Recommendation: **"To push back"** or **"retracting"**

- **Anheften** → *To suture* OR *To affix* OR *To anchor to*
  - Surgical context referring to attaching valve to annulus
  - Recommendation: **"To affix to"** or **"to suture to"**

- **Herausziehen** → *To retract* / *To withdraw* / *To pull back*
  - Device deployment context
  - Recommendation: **"To retract"** (most precise in medical context)

- **Formgedächtnislegierung** → *Shape memory alloy*
  - Standard technical term; often abbreviated SMA

- **NiTiNol** → *NiTiNol* (no translation; keep as is)
  - Nickel-Titanium alloy

- **Elastizität** / **elastisch** → *Elasticity* / *Elastic*
  - Standard term

- **Federelemente** → *Spring elements* OR *Spring mechanisms*
  - Mechanical components
  - Recommendation: **"Spring elements"** (preserves the German concept)

- **Ösen** → *Eyelets* OR *Loops* OR *Eye-loops*
  - Small attachment points
  - Recommendation: **"Eyelets"** (more precise in mechanical/device context)

- **Schlaufen** → *Loops* / *Loop elements*
  - Similar to Ösen but larger
  - Sometimes combined: "ösenförmige Schlaufen" = *eyelet-forming loops* or *loop eyelets*

### **Mechanical Properties and Actions**
- **Selbstexpansion** / **selbstexpandierbar** → *Self-expansion* / *Self-expanding*
  - Standard medical device terminology

- **Vorspannung** → *Preload* / *Pre-tension* OR *Prestressing*
  - Context: radial outward force in compressed state
  - Recommendation: **"Radial preload"** or **"prestressing force"**

- **spiralartig** / **spiralförmig** → *Helically* / *Spiral* OR *Spiraling*
  - Describes the rolling motion
  - Recommendation: **"Spiraling outward"** / **"to spiral outward"**

- **Mozartlocke** → *Mozart curl* (idiomatic; keep as-is with explanation)
  - Descriptive term for the coiled leaflet appearance
  - Recommendation: Keep German term with description: *"Mozart curl (spiraled leaflet configuration)"*

### **Device Attachment Points**
- **Befestigungselemente** → *Attachment elements* OR *Fixation elements*
  - General term for attachment structures
  - Recommendation: **"Attachment elements"** (broader); **"fixation elements"** (more specific for permanent fixation)

- **Abdichtungsmembran** → *Sealing membrane* OR *Skirt*
  - Device component between valve and stent
  - Recommendation: **"Sealing membrane"** or **"skirt"** (common TAVI term for similar structures)

## Notes for Translator

1. **Consistency is critical**: The terms "Mitralklappenstent," "Stentbügel," and "Verankerungselemente" appear throughout. Maintain consistent translations.

2. **Technical vs. Clinical**: This patent balances technical device engineering with clinical application. Use more technical terminology in device descriptions (sections describing structure) and more clinical terminology in procedure sections.

3. **Compound Terms**: German frequently uses compound terms (e.g., "Mitralklappensegel"). English translations should preserve the semantic relationship (e.g., "anterior mitral leaflet" not "anterior valve leaflet").

4. **Anatomical Directions**: Proximal/distal, anterior/posterior, atrial/ventricular, lateral/medial are absolute and should not be translated—they describe anatomical relationships.

5. **Abbreviations**: Watch for in-text abbreviations (AMKS, PMKS, LA, LV, CT, SAM). Maintain parallel abbreviations in English translation (AMKS→AML for Anterior Mitral Leaflet, if desired, but original abbreviations are language-neutral).

6. **Latin Terms**: The document uses both German and Latin terms (Chordae tendineae). In English, the Latin term is preferred in technical contexts.

7. **Device Behavior**: Terms like "einrollen" describe a specific mechanical behavior. The spiraling/curling action should be consistently translated to maintain reader understanding of the innovation.
