## Translation Considerations

### 1. **Terminology Consistency**
- **"Verfahren"** can mean either "method," "procedure," or "process." For consistency in this patent, suggest using **"method"** when referring to the overall planning approach and **"procedure"** when describing execution steps.
- **"Prozedur"** should consistently translate to **"procedure"** to maintain distinction from "Verfahren."

### 2. **English Loanwords**
- The original German text already contains "Engineering" and "System" as English words. These should remain unchanged in translation.

### 3. **Compound Words**
- German allows extensive compounding. Many English translations will necessarily be longer (e.g., "Planungskonfigurationsstrukturbibliothek" → "Planning configuration structure library"). Consider hyphenation for readability: "Planning-configuration-structure library" if needed.

### 4. **Grammatical Forms**
- Terms like "ausgebildet" (formed/configured), "gebildet" (formed), and "definiert" (defined) appear frequently as past participles used adjectivally. Ensure consistent translation of these forms in context.
