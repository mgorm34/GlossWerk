## TERMINOLOGY PATTERNS & RECOMMENDATIONS

### Recurring Patterns in This Patent

1. **Compound nouns with system architecture**: `*-komponenten`, `*-bibliothek`, `*-struktur`, `*-schritt`
   - Recommended strategy: Always maintain compound translation; decomposing loses technical precision
   - Examples: "Planungskomponenten" → "planning components", "Simulationsbibliothek" → "simulation library"

2. **Process terminology**: `Schritt`, `Verfahren`, `Prozedur`, `Prozess`
   - Context matters: 'step' for process sequence, 'procedure' for formal methodology, 'process' for general flow, 'method' for algorithm
   - Recommendation: Use sparingly synonymized; pick one and stick throughout

3. **Manufacturing/assembly domain**: `Montage`, `Produktionszellen`, `Zykluszeiten`, `Roboterwerkzeugen`
   - These are domain-specific; use standard manufacturing English equivalents consistently

4. **Substructure/configuration hierarchy**: `Substrukturen`, `Auswahlsubstrukturen`, `Zielsubstrukturen`
   - Recommendation: Use "substructures" consistently; treat as architectural term, not literal decomposition

5. **Library/database terminology**: `*-bibliothek` compounds
   - All translate to "[noun] library"; maintain parallel structure

6. **Optimization/AI terminology**: `Optimierung`, `Inferenz`, `Weiterbildung`, `Metaheuristische`
   - These are technical/algorithmic; keep as standard English terms

### Quality Assurance Notes

- **Watch out for**: Compound nouns that appear only in variants (singular/plural/genitive) — ensure stem form translation is consistent
- **Flag verbs** that may appear frequently (e.g., "bestimmen", "umfassen") — these may need context-dependent translation and should NOT be added to the glossary
- **Loanwords**: "Engineering", "System", "Modelle", "Informationen", "Technologien" are already English; keep as-is
- **Patent formality**: Prefer formal patent terminology ("plurality" over "multiple", "subject matter" over "object") where established conventions exist
